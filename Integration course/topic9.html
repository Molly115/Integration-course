Validation & Writing Conventions

Ensuring code conformance and professional content standards.

W3C Validation Services

W3C provides free services to check if your website follows "Open Standards."

  • Purpose: Evaluates HTML, XHTML, and CSS code for errors.
  • Conformance: Helps ensure your site works consistently across all browsers.
  • Learning Tool: Web designers use it to evaluate and learn from other sites.

Service Security

Sometimes these services are abused by third parties, but modest traffic is normal.

  • Blocking: You can block validators using IP addresses or User-Agent headers.
  • Caution: Ensure no one in your own organization is using the tool before blocking it.

Professional Writing Conventions

Beyond code, the text on your website must follow specific rules to help readers understand your message.

Spelling All words must be spelled correctly, including tricky homophones.
Punctuation Correct use of full stops, commas in lists, and apostrophes.
Capitalization Required for proper nouns and the start of sentences.
Grammar Strict adherence to subject-verb agreement and correct tenses.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Overly Wordy Sentences: Keep your content concise for web readers.
  • Poor Paragraphing: Break text into small, readable chunks.
  • Genre Specifics: Remember that different types of writing (like scripts vs. articles) have different rules.
